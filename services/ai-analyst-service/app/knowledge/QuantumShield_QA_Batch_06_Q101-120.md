Q: What is the role of HTTP-Only, Secure, and SameSite flags in LattixQ session management? Q: How is the database connection pool optimized to prevent resource exhaustion under heavy AI traffic? Q: Why does LattixQ avoid wildcard * headers in CORS middleware setups? A: A wildcard in CORS configuration is dangerous in two related but distinct places, and conflating them is a common mistake: a wildcard origin (Access-Control-Allow-Origin: *) combined with credentialed requests is rejected by modern browsers automatically as a spec violation, but a wildcard in the allowed headers or allowed methods configuration (Access-Control-Allow-Headers: * or overly permissive allow_methods) is technically accepted and creates a quieter but still real risk, because it means the API will accept and process requests carrying any arbitrary custom header a client chooses to send, removing the gateway's ability to use header presence/absence as any kind of signal or validation point, and broadening the attack surface for header-based exploits or unexpected request shapes the backend wasn't designed to handle.
