### Q: How does Shor's algorithm break RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC)?
A: Shor's algorithm is a quantum algorithm that solves the prime factorization problem (for RSA) and the discrete logarithm problem (for Diffie-Hellman and ECC) in polynomial time, $O((\log N)^3)$. Classical computers require exponential time to solve these problems. ### Q: How does Grover's algorithm impact symmetric key algorithms like AES?
A: Grover's algorithm is a quantum search algorithm that solves unstructured search problems in quadratic time, $O(\sqrt{N})$. For instance, AES-128 is reduced to 64 bits of security, making it vulnerable to quantum brute-force searches. ### Q: What is the differences between ML-DSA (FIPS 204) and SLH-DSA (FIPS 205) signature schemes?

### Q: What is the role of State-Based Signature schemes like XMSS and LMS?
A: State-based signature schemes, such as LMS (RFC 8554) and XMSS (RFC 8391), are early post-quantum digital signature standards approved by NIST (NIST SP 800-208) for specific use cases. They rely on Merkle trees and one-time signatures. Because they are stateful, the signer must maintain a non-repeating state index to avoid reusing a private key leaf, which would compromise the security.
